The Plan at a Glance

Two Hill Stations, One Epic Trip

Munnar (south Kerala, near Kochi) and Wayanad (north Kerala, near Kozhikode) are ~205 km apart. We enter from Kochi by train, explore Munnar's tea country, ride north to Wayanad, then loop back home from Kozhikode — the cheapest, most scenic circuit.

🚆
Train In & OutSabari Exp to Kochi, return from Kozhikode — sleeper class
🛵
Rented BikesScooters & bikes in both hills for freedom + savings
🏡
HomestaysClean budget homestays, ~₹1,300/night for 4
🌧️
Monsoon MagicJuly = lush green, full waterfalls, off-season rates
⚠️ July is peak monsoon in Kerala. Expect heavy rain, slippery roads & occasional landslide closures in Munnar/Wayanad ghats. Carry rain gear, ride slowly, check weather & road status daily, and keep the itinerary flexible. Rates are lowest now — that's the budget win.

Get Around

Bike & Transport Rentals 🛵

Renting 2 two-wheelers for 4 people is the cheapest, most flexible way to explore the hills. Carry your driving licence; helmets are a must (and the law).

🛵 Scooter / Bike Rental

Scooter (Activa) ₹400–500/day · Geared bike (Pulsar/Himalayan) ₹700–900/day. Petrol extra (~₹110/L). Need: licence + ₹1,000 deposit / ID.

Munnar: 2 bikes ×3 days ≈ ₹3,000 + fuel ₹1,500
Wayanad: 2 scooters ×2 days ≈ ₹1,600 + fuel ₹900

🚌 Buses & Jeeps

KSRTC buses connect everything cheaply. Shared 4WD jeeps for Kolukkumalai & safaris. Local autos for short hops (₹50–150).

Kochi→Munnar bus: ₹600 (4) Munnar→Wayanad bus: ₹2,200 (4) Wayanad→Kozhikode bus: ₹400 (4)
🌧️ Monsoon riding: roads are wet & foggy with hairpin bends. Ride slow, avoid night riding, keep raincoats & phone in a waterproof pouch. If a ghat road is closed, switch to bus/jeep.

🍺 Alcohol Notes

Kerala sells liquor mainly through state "Bevco" shops & bar-hotels — expect queues. Toddy shops serve cheap local palm wine with spicy food (a fun, authentic experience). Beer ~₹150–200, IMFL quarter ~₹150–250. Budget set aside: ~₹6,000 for the group across the trip (≈₹150–200 pp on drinking days). Never drink & ride — use a bus/auto.

Be Prepared

Monsoon Packing & Smart Tips 🎒

  • 🧥
    Rain gear first: raincoat/poncho, quick-dry clothes, waterproof pouch for phone/cash, dry-bag for the backpack.
  • 👟
    Grippy footwear: trekking shoes/sandals with good grip — trails & waterfalls get very slippery.
  • 🩹
    Leech & first-aid: carry salt/Odomos for leeches on Wayanad treks, plus basic meds & motion-sickness tablets for ghat roads.
  • 📄
    Documents: ID proof + driving licence (for bike rental), printed train tickets, offline maps downloaded.
  • 💵
    Cash: carry enough — many homestays, toddy shops, falls & jeeps are cash-only; ATMs are sparse in hills.
  • 📅
    Book early: reserve trains ~60 days ahead; confirm Eravikulam & Chembra permits and check daily road/weather status.
  • 🔋
    Power & signal: power bank essential; mobile signal is patchy in estates & forests. Inform family of your plan.
  • 🌦️
    Stay flexible: have a Plan B indoor option (Tea Museum, cafes, local markets) for the heaviest rain days.
